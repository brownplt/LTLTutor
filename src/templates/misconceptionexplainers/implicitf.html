Finally Review

In LTL, the formula F p asserts that p will eventually be true. This means that p can be true in the current state, but it doesn't have to be. What is required is that p becomes true at least once in any future state, including potentially the present state.
Importantly, F p doesn't specify when p will become true, only that it will definitely become true. Thus both these traces satisfy F p: 

 
            flowchart LR;
            n1["!p"]-->n2["p"];
            n2["p"]-->n2["p"];  
        
 
            flowchart LR;
            n1["!p"]-->n2["!p"];
            n2["!p"]-->n3["!p"];
            n3["!p"]-->n4["p"];
            n4["p"]-->n3["!p"];
