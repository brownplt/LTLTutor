Finally Review

It may be the case that you believe the F (finally) operator implicitly applies at some state, meaning it will automatically trigger at a particular point in time. However, the F operator has a different meaning in LTL. It asserts that p will eventually be true, but it doesn't specify \emph{when}.
What is required is that p becomes true at least once in any future state, including potentially the present state. Importantly, F p doesn't specify when p will become true, only that it will definitely become true. Thus both these traces satisfy F p: 

 
            flowchart LR;
            n1["!p"]-->n2["p"];
            n2["p"]-->n2["p"];  
        
 
            flowchart LR;
            n1["!p"]-->n2["!p"];
            n2["!p"]-->n3["!p"];
            n3["!p"]-->n4["p"];
            n4["p"]-->n3["!p"];
