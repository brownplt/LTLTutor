Next Review

Some learners mistakenly believe that the next (X) operator in LTL can apply to multiple states or spread its effect across a range of states. However, this is not true. The X operator is very precise: it applies strictly to the immediate next state and nothing else.

For example, the formula X (X p) means that p holds two states from now. It does not imply anything about p holding in the current state, the immediate next state, or any state beyond the two-step distance specified.

Another way to think of this is that the next operator does not fan out its effect; it's not about a condition holding over a range of states or eventually. It applies strictly to the very next state.
