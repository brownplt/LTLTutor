Review: Next (X)

In LTL, the formula X p asserts that p will be true in the next state. Remember, the next (X) operator is very precise: it only refers to the immediately next state, not multiple states ahead or the current state. For instance, the formula X (X p) means p holds two states from now, and doesn't imply anything about p holding in the current state, the next state, or three states from now. Another way to think of this is that the next operator does not fan out its effect; it's not about a condition holding over a range of states or eventually. It applies strictly to the very next state.
