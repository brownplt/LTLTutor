Until Review

You may think that if x is always true, the formula x U y ("x until y") is always satisfied, even if y is never true.

In reality, x U y states two things:

  • x holds at every moment in time until y becomes true. This means the x must be true at the current state.
  • y will eventually become true at some point in the future.
What this means is that if y never becomes true, then x U y is false, regardless of whether x holds for all preceding moments.
