Tip: Be cautious of implicit assumptions

When interpreting LTL formulae, it's essential to be cautious of not allowing implicit assumptions to color your understanding of what the formula explicitly states. LTL is a precise language used to define specific behaviors and conditions over time, and each operator and syntax has a clear definition and function. It's important to interpret these formulas based on what is directly presented, not what we might assume or infer beyond the text. For instance, if you come across a formula like G (p -> q), it is easy to implicitly infer properties that aren't supported by the formula. For example, one might assume that q must always follow p or that p triggers q. However, the formula itself doesn't explicitly state these conditions. It only says that if p is true at any point, then q must also be true at that point.
