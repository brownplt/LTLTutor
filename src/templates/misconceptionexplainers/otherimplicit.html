Tip: Be cautious of implicit assumptions Tip

You may be tempted to make implicit assumptions about LTL formulae, based on your understanding of the system or the context in which the formula is used. Unlike natural language, however, LTL is not open to interpretation or ambiguity. It is a precise language used to define specific behaviors and conditions over time. Each operator and syntax has a clear definition and function, and does not encode implicit meanings or assumptions.
For instance, if you come across a formula like G (p -> q), it is easy to implicitly infer properties that aren't supported by the formula. For example, one might assume that q must always follow p or that p triggers q. However, the formula itself doesn't explicitly state these conditions. It only says that if p is true at any point, then q must also be true at that point.
